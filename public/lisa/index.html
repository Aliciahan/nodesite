"Principles of Graphic Design."

Same as artists, graphic designers also have a canvas you can put your brilliant ideas on. Graphic design includes varieties of basic visual elements, we call it principles of graphic design. The three main components of graphic design are point, line and surface. A Graphic designer’s job is to start with these three basic visual elements, through aesthetic law and visual psychology to establish a visual language and deliver a message to the audience.

This site has been designed for those who are interested in graphic design. You can get a general idea of what visual language is through playing the little graphic design game. In addition, this site allows you to experience the creative thinking process of graphic designers.

You can always switch the button located bottom left when you are playing this site. This button allows you to switch site style. The relationship between black and white in graphic design not only uses two colors but also controls the overall visual effect. Through this little button you need to consider the size, shape, position, balance, also echoes between negative and positive space.

Scale

Scale is a concept of relative size. The function of scale in graphic design is to create a hierarchy between other visual elements and background. Scale is widely use in typography. Large scale represents an emphasis, small scale tells a metaphor.

©1957, Daniel Barkle

Repetition and rhythm

Repetition
Repetition is a very common form of visualisation that can be seen everywhere in humanities and nature. In graphic design, the repetitive arrangement of the same shape is a commonly used method. Can make the design object achieve a harmonious, unified visual effects.

©️1975, 福田 繁雄（ふくだ しげお)


Rhythm
Rhythm not only exists in music. In graphic design ideas are also common. The key of graphic deign and music is mutual transformation, there is no distinction between aesthetics. In terms of music itself, it is taking advantage of the time interval, the strength of the sound shows a regular repetition. However, in graphic design, similar graphic elements appear in different forms can give a sense of unification and variation. This creates a sense of rhythm.

©️1953, Josef Müller-Brockmann

Symmetry and Asymmetry

Symmetry
Symmetry means that objects are lined up in the same way on both sides of the centre line. In design concept, we can divide symmetry into two-forms, relative symmetry and absolute symmetry. Relative symmetry means the graphic elements on either side of the centre line not need be exactly the same. But after a certain adjustment can still overlap with the other side. In graphic design symmetry gives a sense of balance and order. Usually symmetry is also a composition technique, which can apply to typography and layout design.

©️1977, Mike Joyce


Asymmetry
Compared with symmetry, asymmetry is a very complicated graphic design technique. But it can often help to create a stunning design idea. It breaks the limitations of symmetric design. Designer places the visual emphasis deviation from the visual centre, Attracting the audience by deliberate asymmetry.

©️1990, Mike Joyce

Diffusion and Grid
Diffusion and Grid both are the ways of categorising graphic elements.

Diffusion
Diffusion is arranging the visual elements from the visual centre gradually to the side, showing a gradual change in the transition. To convey a type of stage order.

©️1978, Swissted.


Grid
Grid systems are the core of graphic design. It was first proposed by Josef Müller-Brockmann in his objective typography and design concept. Grid systems are the powerful tools to assist in the development of graphic design. It can create a sense of hierarchy and modular management of visual elements. It also makes the content more readable, and has a better visual communication experience with the audience.

Tangent and overlap
Tangent and overlap both are the positional relationship of the visual elements.

Tangent
There is only one intersection point between two or more visual elements. It forms a close relationship between visual elements. And increases the link between graphics.

©️1971, Swissted.


Overlap
Overlap simply means two or more graphics are stacked together. On the one hand, overlapping makes visual elements interrelated. On the other hand, when two visual elements overlapped, the third elements which are the intersecting parts will appear and form a positive and negative relationship between each other.

©️1972, Mike Joyce.

Modularity
Modularity design refers to the whole visual elements that are made up with small pieces of modules. Different modules form a specific functional area, works rationally and orderly. Newspaper is one of the best cases of the practical application of modularity; same page, but different area tells completely different story and easy to identify. Modularity also widely used in modern graphic design. Such as bootstrap structure in web design.

How to play
This activity was originally designed by Swiss graphic design educator Armin Hofmann in his book “Graphic design manual”. The aim was to deliver a holistic thinking creation method.

©️1963, Armin Hofmann.

“by blanking out single parts, figures are obtained which more or less suggest the basic form” Hofmann, A.(1965)
